Non-Parametric Derivative Estimation With Penalized B-Splines: Asymptotics and An Improved Estimator

Authors: Christopher Odoom, John Staudenmayer and Bright Antwi Boasiako

Keywords: MISE, P-splines, smoothing parameter estimation

Abstract

Penalized splines (P-Splines) are an effective and flexible nonparametric regression method. Adequate performance requires a carefully chosen smoothing parameter. Most smoothing parameter selection methods in the literature are developed for the case when the mean regression function is of interest, and less attention has been paid to the case when a derivative of the regression function is of interest. This paper contributes to the latter case in two ways. First, we derive the asymptotic rates of convergence for the P-Spline derivative estimator and show that differentiating the estimate of the mean regression function, with the parameters (including the smoothing parameter) held fixed, can achieve an optimal L2 rate of convergence. Additionally, we propose a new method that improves upon that estimator by finding a smoothing parameter that minimizes an estimate of the finite sample mean integrated squared error of the derivative. A simulation experiment compares the proposed estimator to the one mentioned above and methods in the literature and indicates better finite sample performance for the proposed method. An application to real data illustrates the use of our proposed methodology.
