Derivative estimation based on difference sequence via locally weighted least squares regression (2015)

Authors: Wang, W. W., & Lin, L.

Journal: The Journal of Machine Learning Research

Year: 2015

Citations: 45+

Download PDF

Summary

This paper presents an innovative approach to derivative estimation using difference sequences combined with locally weighted least squares regression. The method provides an alternative to traditional kernel-based and spline-based approaches.

Key Contributions

  • Novel difference sequence methodology
  • Efficient computational algorithm
  • Theoretical analysis of convergence rates
  • Comparative analysis with existing methods

Methodology

The authors develop their approach through:

  1. Construction of difference sequences
  2. Local polynomial fitting
  3. Adaptive bandwidth selection

The key innovation lies in combining difference sequences with local fitting to improve efficiency.

Theoretical Results

Key convergence result:

|f̂'(x) - f'(x)| = O_p((nh³)^(-1/2) + h²)

where:

  • h: bandwidth parameter
  • n: sample size
  • f': true derivative
  • f̂': estimated derivative

Comparison with P-splines

Advantages over P-splines:

  • No need for knot selection
  • Local adaptivity
  • Simpler theoretical analysis

Disadvantages:

  • Higher computational cost
  • Less smooth estimates
  • Boundary effects

Relevance to Current Research

This work provides:

  • Alternative perspective on derivative estimation
  • Comparative benchmark for P-spline methods
  • Insights into bandwidth selection
