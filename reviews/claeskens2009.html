Asymptotic properties of penalized spline estimators (2009)

Authors: Claeskens, G., Krivobokova, T., & Opsomer, J. D.

Journal: Journal of Statistical Planning and Inference

Year: 2009

Citations: 500+

Download PDF

Summary

This paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the asymptotic behavior of penalized spline estimators. The authors establish theoretical foundations for understanding convergence rates under different knot selection regimes.

Key Contributions

  • Rigorous analysis of asymptotic properties
  • Distinction between small and large knots regimes
  • Derivation of optimal convergence rates
  • Framework for understanding smoothing parameter selection

Methodology

The paper's analytical approach includes:

  1. Analysis of different knot selection regimes
  2. Development of asymptotic theory for P-splines
  3. Investigation of smoothing parameter behavior

A key insight is the identification of two distinct regimes based on the number of knots relative to sample size.

Relevance to Current Research

This work is fundamental to our research as it provides the theoretical framework for understanding convergence rates in derivative estimation. Their analysis of knot selection regimes directly influences our approach to optimal parameter selection.

Critical Analysis

Strengths:

  • Rigorous mathematical analysis
  • Clear distinction of asymptotic regimes
  • Comprehensive theoretical framework

Limitations:

  • Limited discussion of practical implementation
  • Focus on asymptotic rather than finite sample properties
