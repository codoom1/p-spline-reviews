Bandwidth Choice and Confidence Intervals for Derivatives of Noisy Data (1987)

Authors: Müller, H. G., Stadtmüller, U., & Schmitt, T.

Journal: Biometrika

Year: 1987

Citations: 600+

View PDF

Summary

This seminal paper addresses two fundamental challenges in derivative estimation: automatic bandwidth selection and construction of confidence intervals. It establishes foundational principles that influence modern approaches to parameter selection in both kernel and spline-based methods.

Key Contributions

  • Automatic bandwidth selection method
  • Construction of confidence intervals
  • Analysis of finite sample properties
  • Monte Carlo validation

Methodology

The authors develop their methodology through:

  1. Analysis of optimal bandwidth rates
  2. Plug-in bandwidth estimation
  3. Confidence interval construction

The key innovation lies in connecting bandwidth selection to confidence interval coverage.

Theoretical Results

Optimal bandwidth rate:

h_opt ∼ n^(-1/(2r+2m+1))

where:

  • n: sample size
  • r: derivative order
  • m: kernel order

Relevance to P-splines

While focused on kernel methods, this work influences P-spline methodology through:

  • Understanding of smoothing parameter selection
  • Trade-off between bias and variance
  • Confidence interval construction principles

Impact on Current Research

This paper continues to influence modern derivative estimation through:

  • Parameter selection principles
  • Uncertainty quantification methods
  • Finite sample performance analysis

Practical Implications

Key practical insights include:

  • Data-driven parameter selection
  • Balance between smoothing and precision
  • Importance of uncertainty assessment
