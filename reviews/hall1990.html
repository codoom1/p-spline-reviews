Asymptotically optimal difference-based estimation of variance in nonparametric regression

Authors: Peter Hall, J. W. Kay, D. M. Titterington

Year: 1990

Journal: Biometrika

Summary

This paper introduces and analyzes difference-based methods for estimating variance in nonparametric regression settings. The authors develop asymptotically optimal difference sequences for variance estimation, providing both theoretical foundations and practical implementations.

Key Contributions

  • Develops a theoretical framework for difference-based variance estimation
  • Derives optimal difference sequences for minimizing asymptotic variance
  • Demonstrates that simple difference sequences can achieve near-optimal efficiency
  • Provides practical guidelines for implementing difference-based variance estimators

Methodology

The paper focuses on difference-based estimators of the form:

  • Uses sequences of difference coefficients to estimate variance
  • Derives optimality conditions for these sequences
  • Analyzes the asymptotic properties of the estimators
  • Compares various difference sequences in terms of efficiency

Key Findings

  • Simple difference sequences (like first-order differences) can achieve near-optimal efficiency
  • The optimal difference sequence depends on the smoothness of the regression function
  • Higher-order differences can improve efficiency but may be more sensitive to outliers
  • Provides practical recommendations for choosing difference orders in applications

Impact and Applications

This work has become fundamental in nonparametric regression, particularly for:

  • Variance estimation in nonparametric smoothing
  • Bandwidth selection in kernel regression
  • Diagnostic checking in nonparametric modeling
  • Development of adaptive smoothing procedures
