Bandwidth choice for differentiation (1986)

Author: Rice, J. A.

Journal: Journal of Multivariate Analysis

Year: 1986

Citations: 450+

Download PDF

Summary

This foundational paper proposes a class of procedures for choosing the bandwidth or smoothing parameter in nonparametric derivative estimation. The work establishes fundamental principles that influence modern parameter selection methods across different estimation approaches.

Key Contributions

  • Systematic approach to bandwidth selection
  • Nearly unbiased error estimation
  • Theoretical analysis of convergence rates
  • Extension to higher-order derivatives

Methodology

The paper develops its methodology through:

  1. Analysis of integrated mean square error
  2. Development of error estimators
  3. Study of tapered Fourier series estimates

The key innovation lies in connecting bandwidth selection to minimizing an unbiased estimate of the error.

Theoretical Results

Key theoretical result:

IMSE = n⁻¹h⁻(2r+1)R(K) + h²(s-r)B²(f)

where:

  • h: bandwidth parameter
  • r: derivative order
  • s: function smoothness
  • R(K): kernel-dependent constant
  • B²(f): squared bias term

Relevance to Modern Methods

This work remains relevant to current research through:

  • Parameter selection principles for P-splines
  • Error assessment methodology
  • Understanding of bias-variance trade-offs
  • Extension to adaptive methods

Connection to P-splines

While focused on kernel methods, the principles apply to P-splines through:

  • Similar bias-variance decomposition
  • Error measure optimization
  • Adaptive parameter selection

Impact on Current Research

Key implications for modern methods:

  • Foundation for automatic parameter selection
  • Framework for error assessment
  • Basis for adaptive procedures
