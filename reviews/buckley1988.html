The estimation of residual variance in nonparametric regression (1988)

Authors: Buckley, M. J., Eagleson, G. K., & Silverman, B. W.

Journal: Biometrika

Year: 1988

Citations: 250+

Download PDF

Summary

This paper addresses the fundamental problem of estimating residual variance in nonparametric regression. The authors develop methods for variance estimation that are particularly relevant to derivative estimation and bandwidth selection problems.

Key Contributions

  • Novel approach to residual variance estimation in nonparametric settings
  • Development of consistent estimators under minimal assumptions
  • Analysis of bias and variance trade-offs
  • Application to bandwidth selection problems

Methodology

The authors develop their methodology through:

  1. Difference-based estimators for residual variance
  2. Analysis of optimal difference sequences
  3. Extensions to heteroscedastic settings

A key innovation is their treatment of boundary effects and their impact on variance estimation.

Relevance to Current Research

This work is particularly relevant to our research in:

  • Understanding error structure in derivative estimation
  • Improving bandwidth selection methods
  • Handling heteroscedastic errors in practice

Critical Analysis

Strengths:

  • Strong theoretical foundations
  • Practical implementation guidelines
  • Clear connections to bandwidth selection

Limitations:

  • Assumes equally spaced design points
  • Limited treatment of correlated errors

Implementation Details

Key variance estimator form:

σ̂² = (2(n-r))⁻¹ Σᵢ(ΔʳYᵢ)²

where:

  • Δʳ: r-th order difference operator
  • Yᵢ: observed data points
  • n: sample size
  • r: difference order
