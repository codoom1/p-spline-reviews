Asymptotic theory of penalized splines (2019)

Author: Luo Xiao

Journal: Electronic Journal of Statistics

Year: 2019

Citations: 40+

Download PDF

Summary

This paper provides a comprehensive treatment of asymptotic theory for penalized splines, establishing L∞ and L₂ convergence rates and demonstrating rate optimality under various conditions.

Key Contributions

  • Unified asymptotic theory for penalized splines
  • Proof of rate optimality
  • Analysis of local asymptotics
  • Extension to derivative estimation

Methodology

The paper develops theory through:

  1. Analysis of convergence rates in different norms
  2. Investigation of local asymptotic behavior
  3. Study of boundary effects

Key innovation lies in the unified treatment of different asymptotic regimes.

Theoretical Results

Key convergence result:

||f̂ - f||∞ = O_p(n^(-r/(2r+1)))

where:

  • f̂: penalized spline estimator
  • f: true function
  • r: smoothness parameter
  • n: sample size

Relevance to Current Research

This work is fundamental to understanding:

  • Theoretical behavior of derivative estimators
  • Optimal convergence rates
  • Selection of tuning parameters

Critical Analysis

Strengths:

  • Rigorous mathematical treatment
  • Comprehensive theoretical framework
  • Clear connections to practical applications

Limitations:

  • Limited practical implementation guidance
  • Focus on asymptotic rather than finite sample properties
  • Restricted to univariate setting
