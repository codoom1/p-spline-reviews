On mean derivative estimation of longitudinal and functional data: from sparse to dense

Authors: Hassan Sharghi Ghale-Joogh and S. Mohammad E. Hosseini-Nasab

Year: 2021

Source: Statistical Papers

View Original Source

Review

This paper addresses the challenging problem of derivative estimation in longitudinal and functional data settings, with particular attention to the transition between sparse and dense sampling scenarios. The work provides theoretical foundations and practical methodology for mean derivative estimation across different sampling regimes.

Key Contributions

  • Unified framework for sparse and dense functional data
  • Novel approach to mean derivative estimation
  • Theoretical analysis of convergence rates
  • Efficient computational algorithms

Methodology

The approach encompasses:

  • Adaptive smoothing techniques
  • Cross-validation for parameter selection
  • Mixed-effects modeling integration
  • Bootstrap-based inference

Relevance to P-spline Methods

This work complements our P-spline research through:

  • Treatment of longitudinal data structures
  • Handling of irregular sampling
  • Parameter selection strategies
  • Theoretical insights for derivative estimation

Practical Impact

The methodology has been successfully applied to:

  • Growth curve analysis
  • Biomedical longitudinal studies
  • Economic time series
  • Environmental monitoring data
